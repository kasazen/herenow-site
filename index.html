What we do

We spend two weeks inside your company's actual data. Contracts, Slack, invoices, tooling — the real material, not a survey. We come back with a short written account of where AI changes your numbers, and where it doesn't.

What you walk away with

A document. Not slides, not a dashboard, not a Notion page that decays the week after we leave. Twenty pages of prose, written for the executive who has to act on it. We sit with you for an afternoon and walk every recommendation in the room.

What comes after

The audit is the engagement. It either ends there, with a memo you can act on yourself, or it doesn't — and we keep going. Most of the time it doesn't end. The companies who say yes to the audit usually have one or two things in their business that an off-the-shelf tool will never fit, and those are the things we end up building together.

Who tends to reach out

Owners and operators of companies between twenty‑five and a few hundred people. Usually profitable, usually a few years past the founding scramble, usually with a year of half‑finished AI experiments behind them and a quiet anxiety that they're already behind.
